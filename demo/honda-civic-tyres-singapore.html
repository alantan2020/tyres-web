Honda Civic Tyre Size & Recommended Tyres in Singapore

The Honda Civic Gen 11 FE (2022–2026) uses 235/40R18 tyres as standard across all variants including the e:HEV hybrid. The older Gen 10 FC (2016–2021) uses 215/50R17 on the 1.5T Turbo and 215/55R16 on the 1.8 i-VTEC base model. All generations share the 5×114.3 bolt pattern. Replacement tyres in Singapore start from $110 per tyre installed for 215/50R17 and from $155 for 235/40R18.

Buying Guide — Which Tyre is Right for Your Civic?

Gen 11 FE (2022–2026) owners

All FE variants use 235/40R18, a performance-oriented low-profile size. For Singapore's wet roads, prioritise a tyre with A-rated wet grip (shown on EU tyre label). The Michelin Pilot Sport 4 and Continental SportContact 7 are top picks if you value handling; the Bridgestone Turanza T005 is the go-to if you want a quieter, longer-lasting option.

Gen 10 FC (2016–2021) 1.5T owners

The 1.5T Turbo OE size 215/50R17 offers the best balance of ride comfort and handling. If you want to save money, the alternative 215/55R16 (same diameter, 16-inch wheels) is fully compatible and typically $10–$20 cheaper per tyre. The Bridgestone Ecopia EP300 is our top recommendation for daily commuting — excellent wet grip and fuel efficiency suited to Singapore driving.

Gen 10 FC 1.8 i-VTEC owners

The 1.8 base model uses 215/55R16. This is one of the most common sizes in Singapore, so you'll have the widest selection and lowest prices. Budget tyres like the Hankook Kinergy Eco 2 or Maxxis MAP5 are perfectly adequate for the lower-powered 1.8 engine and are popular among Grab and private hire drivers for their mileage value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What tyre size does the Honda Civic use in Singapore? ▾

It depends on the generation and variant:

  • Gen 11 FE (2022–2026): all variants including the e:HEV hybrid use 235/40R18
  • Gen 10 FC (2016–2021) 1.5T Turbo: OE size 215/50R17 (can also fit 215/55R16)
  • Gen 10 FC (2016–2021) 1.8 i-VTEC: OE size 215/55R16
  • Gen 10 FC Facelift Sport (2019–2021): 235/40R18

All generations use the 5×114.3 bolt pattern with a 64.1mm centre bore.

How much does it cost to replace tyres on a Honda Civic in Singapore? ▾

For 215/50R17: budget brands (Hankook, Maxxis) from $110–$130 per tyre installed; mid-range (Bridgestone, Continental) $145–$175; premium (Michelin, Pirelli) $175–$220.

For 235/40R18: budget from $155; mid-range $195–$230; premium $240–$290.

A full set of 4 mid-range tyres with installation for the 1.5T Turbo (215/50R17) typically costs $580–$700 all-in.

What is the bolt pattern of the Honda Civic FC and FE? ▾

Both the Gen 10 FC (2016–2021) and Gen 11 FE (2022–2026) use the 5×114.3 bolt pattern (also written as 5×4.5"). This is the most common bolt pattern in Singapore, shared by most Honda, Toyota, Mazda, Hyundai, and Kia models. Centre bore is 64.1mm, lug nut thread is M12×1.5, and torque spec is 108 Nm.

Can I fit 215/55R16 tyres on a Honda Civic 1.5T instead of 215/50R17? ▾

Yes. On the Gen 10 Honda Civic FC 1.5T, the OE size is 215/50R17 but fitting 215/55R16 on 16-inch wheels is safe and legal. The overall tyre diameter changes by less than 2% (627mm vs 643mm), which is within the acceptable tolerance for Singapore. You'll benefit from a softer, more comfortable ride and lower tyre cost (starting from $110 vs $120 for 17-inch tyres). Note: you'll need to switch to 16-inch wheels.

How often should I change tyres on my Honda Civic in Singapore? ▾

In Singapore's climate, tyres typically last 3–5 years or 40,000–50,000 km, whichever comes first. Heat and humidity accelerate rubber degradation. Singapore law requires a minimum 1.6mm tread depth; experts recommend replacing at 3mm for safety in heavy rain. Check the DOT code on the tyre sidewall — tyres over 5 years old should be inspected and likely replaced, even if tread looks adequate.

Can I fit aftermarket rims on my Honda Civic FC or FE in Singapore? ▾

Yes. The 5×114.3 bolt pattern is one of the most common in Singapore, giving you a large selection of aftermarket rims from budget to premium. For the Gen 10 FC, popular fitments are 17" (7J–8J, ET35–48) and 18" (7.5J–8.5J, ET35–50). For the Gen 11 FE, most owners stay on 18" to match the OE size. Keep the ET (offset) within ±10mm of OEM to avoid fender rubbing. Entry-level options like Advanti and MSW start from $180/rim; mid-range Enkei and Work from $340–$480/rim; premium Rays Volk TE37 from $1,100/rim.
