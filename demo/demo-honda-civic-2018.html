Frequently Asked Questions

What tyre size does the Honda Civic (2018–2021) use? ▾

The Honda Civic FC (Generation X, 2018–2021) uses different sizes depending on the variant. The 1.5 VTEC Turbo comes with 215/50R17 as standard. The 1.8 i-VTEC base model uses 215/55R16. The 2019 Facelift Sport variant upgrades to 235/40R18. All variants can also accept the other 16" or 17" size as an alternative.

What is the bolt pattern of the Honda Civic FC? ▾

The Honda Civic FC (2016–2021, Generation X) uses a 5×114.3 bolt pattern (also written as 5×4.5"). This is one of the most common bolt patterns in Singapore, shared by most Honda, Toyota, Mazda, Hyundai and Kia models. The centre bore is 64.1mm and the lug nut thread is M12×1.5.

How much does it cost to replace tyres on a Honda Civic in Singapore? ▾

For the 215/50R17 size (most common), budget tyres start from around $110–$130 per tyre installed. Mid-range brands like Bridgestone or Continental cost $160–$200 per tyre. Premium brands like Michelin or Pirelli run $240–$320 per tyre. A full set of 4 tyres mid-range would typically cost $640–$800 all-in with installation.

Can I fit aftermarket rims on my Honda Civic FC? ▾

Yes. The 5×114.3 bolt pattern is extremely common, making the Honda Civic FC one of the easiest cars to find aftermarket rims for. Popular upgrades in Singapore include 17" or 18" rims in widths from 7J to 8.5J. You'll want to keep the ET (offset) between 35–50 to avoid rubbing. Common choices include Enkei RPF1, Work Emotion, and Rays Volk TE37. WhatsApp us for a fitment check on any specific rim.

How often should I change tyres on a Honda Civic in Singapore? ▾

In Singapore's climate, tyres typically last 3–5 years or around 40,000–50,000 km, whichever comes first. The heat and humidity accelerate rubber degradation. Check your tread depth monthly — Singapore law requires a minimum of 1.6mm tread depth. Most experts recommend changing at 3mm for safety in our heavy rain conditions.
